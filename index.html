Constitutional Mandate

National Policy & Legislative Framework

The National Policy on Narcotic Drugs is rooted in Article 47 of the Indian Constitution, directing the State to endeavour to bring about prohibition of the consumption of intoxicating drugs injurious to health.

This constitutional provision is further guided by India's commitment as a signatory to international conventions: Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs, 1961
Convention on Psychotropic Substances, 1971
UN Convention against Illicit Traffic, 1988

Broad Legislative Policy

The legislative framework is contained in three Central Acts:

1940 Drugs & Cosmetics Act
1985 The NDPS Act
1988 Prevention of Illicit Traffic Act

The Narcotics Control Bureau was constituted on 17th March 1986 as the Central Authority under the NDPS Act, 1985 to coordinate drug abuse control functions across various Ministries and Departments.
