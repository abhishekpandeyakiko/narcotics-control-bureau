Constitution & Policy

National Policy

Based on Article 47 of the Indian Constitution, directing the State to bring about prohibition of the consumption, except for medicinal purposes, of intoxicating drugs injurious to health.

UN Conventions 1961, 1971, 1988

Legislative Framework

  • Only Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940
  • The Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985
  • Prevention of Illicit Traffic in NDPS Act, 1988

Journey of Enforcement

Pre-1985

Legislative Foundation

Responsibility of drug abuse control was carried out through various Ministries. The need for a central authority grew with international conventions.

14th Nov, 1985

The NDPS Act

The Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 came into effect, making express provision for constituting a Central Authority.

17th Mar, 1986

Birth of NCB

The Government of India constituted the NARCOTICS CONTROL BUREAU to exercise the powers and functions of the Central Government.

Present

Apex Coordinating Agency

Functioning as the nodal agency for coordination and enforcement through zones across the nation.

Core Functions

Coordination

Coordination of actions by various offices, State Governments, and authorities under the NDPS Act, Customs Act, and other laws.

Implementation

Implementation of obligations in respect of counter-measures against illicit traffic under various international conventions.

Foreign Assistance

Assistance to concerned authorities in foreign countries and international organisations to facilitate universal action.

Inter-Agency Coop

Coordination of actions taken by other concerned Ministries, Departments and Organizations regarding drug abuse.
